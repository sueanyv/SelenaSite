Selena Quintanilla-Pérez

(April 16, 1971 - March 31, 1995)

Selena Quintanilla-Pérez was a Mexican-American singer, songwriter, spokesperson, actress, and fashion designer. She was born in Lake Jackson, TX on April 17, 1971. Her father, Abraham Quintanilla was a member of an American Tejano band called Los Dinos. At the age of ten, Selena began performing with Los Dinos as their lead singer. In 1989 Selena signed with Capitol EMI Latin and released her self-titled debut album the same year. Her 1990 album Ven Conmigo became the first recording by a female Tejano artist to achieve gold status by the Recording Industry Association of America (RIAA). A month after eloping with her lead guitarist Chris Perez on April 2, 1992, Selena released Entre a mi Mundo. The album quickly rose up the charts and was critically acclaimed. In March 1994, she released her fifth studio album Amor Prohibido. (Brennan, n.d.) The album debuted at number three on the US Billboard Top Latin Albums and number one on the US Billboard Regional Mexican. It became the best-selling Tejano albums in the United States. It's success earned Selena her first Grammy nomination and several Latin music awards. In 1995 Selena started preparing an English album with dreams of crossing over in the American market. The singer was also designing her first fashion line and opened several boutiques in Texas.
On March 31, 1995, Selena's story came to a tragic end when her murdered by a family friend. The news of her death made an impact around the world. Her English album Dreaming of you was release posthumous in July 1995. The album sold over 300,000 units in one week, Selena became the third female artist in history with this achievement. ("Selena", n.d.) Although her life was cut short, she has changed the course of Latin music and its influence on American culture. She has influenced singers like Selena Gomez (named after the singer) and Jennifer Lopez. Selena Quintanilla-Pérez will always be known as the Queen of Tejano.
